What's in a name?

Our industry has so many titles and roles, it can make it very difficult when looking for a partner to lead design on your project.

It took me a long time to decipher the different titles but now I am very intentional about the one that I use, so let me break down the various roles.

Here are some of the designer types you might find and what they usually do:

  • Graphic Design: Often a catch-all, it is a form of visual communication which leverages images and other visual elements to convey ideas. They usually focus on designs meant for print rather than digital. They will produce artefacts such as Brand Identities, Marketing materials, product packaging & illustrations.
  • User Experience (UX) Design: Explores the experience people have using a digital product such as an app or website in order to make sure it is easy to use. They will produce artefacts such as user research documents, personas, user journeys and wireframes.
  • User Interface (UI) Design: Defines the look & feel of your product by focussing on color, typography, images, white space & content hierarchy. They will produce artefacts such as design comps in Photoshop or Sketch and can sometimes implement their own designs in code.
  • Interaction (IxD) Design: Focusses on the interaction between the user and the product in very fine detail such as the transition between screens or the animation of an icon when tapped. They will produce things like HTML 5 prototypes or animations using the various prototyping tools now available.
  • Visual Design: Defines the look & feel of your product by translating the brand's visual language and focussing on color, typography, images, white space & content hierarchy.
